Who is Brad D. Strawn?

Brad D. Strawn is a clinical psychologist and theologian at Fuller Theological Seminary. He is known for integrating relational psychoanalysis with Christian theology, emphasizing embodiment, spiritual formation, and emotional healing.

Research Question

How does Brad D. Strawn’s integration of psychoanalytic theory and Christian theology offer new insights into spiritual development and religious experience?

Theoretical Application

Strawn critiques traditional dualism and instead proposes an embodied, relational model of human experience. Using psychoanalytic tools, he shows that spiritual transformation involves emotional healing and relational maturity, not just belief or doctrine.

Critical Analysis

Strawn's approach centers theology on human vulnerability and relationship, offering a counterpoint to overly rational or individualistic models. Some critique his work for risking theological clarity in favor of psychological interpretation, but his insights offer a fuller view of faith formation.

Timeline of Key Works

  • 2012 – The Physical Nature of Christian Life (with Warren Brown)
  • 2014 – Christianity and Psychoanalysis: A New Conversation (edited with Earl Bland)
  • 2020 – Enhancing Christian Life
  • 2022 – Spiritual Diversity in Psychotherapy (editor)

Practical Uses in Ministry and Therapy

In therapy, Strawn’s model supports working with spiritual struggles as real psychological material. In ministry, it frames discipleship as a relational journey that acknowledges wounds and fosters healing. He encourages emotionally aware spiritual leadership and holistic care.
